Andrew J. Sage

Welcome! I am a first year doctoral student in the Department of Statistics at Iowa State University . I am a research assistant in the Center for Survey Statistics and Methodology.

I grew up just east of Cleveland, Ohio and earned my BA in mathematics at The College of Wooster in 2007. I taught high school for four years before deciding to pursue graduate school. I earned my MS in mathematics at Miami University in 2013. I enjoy sharing my interest in mathematics and statistics with others and I am pursuing a career as a college professor.

My research interests are still developing. I am currently working on projects in statistical disclosure limitation and temporal design for sampling and I find both topics interesting. For more information on these projects, please visit my research page.

Besides statistics, I enjoy running, reading mystery novels, rooting for Cleveland sports teams, and spending time with my brothers and sister.
